IMMIGRATION PROCESS FOR INDIANS IN CANADA

🇨🇦 CANADA — WORK IMMIGRATION GUIDE

🔹 1. Overview

Canada is one of the most preferred destinations for Indian professionals due to its high quality of life, immigrant-friendly policies, and strong demand for skilled workers. Key sectors include IT, healthcare, engineering, construction, logistics, and finance. Canada offers both temporary work permits and direct pathways to Permanent Residency (PR), making it one of the easiest countries for long-term settlement.

🔹 2. Types of Work Visas

1. Employer-Specific Work Permit: Most common work visa. Requires job offer from Canadian employer. Usually requires LMIA approval.

2. Open Work Permit: No job offer required. Allows working for any employer. Available for specific categories (spouses, students, etc.).

3. Express Entry (PR Route): Points-based immigration system. Direct pathway to Permanent Residency. Includes Federal Skilled Worker Program.

4. Provincial Nominee Program (PNP): Province-specific immigration programs. Increases chances of PR.

5. Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP): For students who studied in Canada. Allows work up to 3 years.

🔹 3. Eligibility Criteria

  • Relevant educational qualifications
  • Work experience (skilled category preferred)
  • English/French language proficiency (IELTS/CELPIP)
  • Valid job offer (for work permit route)
  • Sufficient funds (for PR pathways)
  • Medical and police clearance

🔹 4. Required Documents

  • Valid passport
  • Educational certificates (ECA required for PR)
  • IELTS/CELPIP score
  • Work experience letters
  • Job offer letter (if applicable)
  • Proof of funds (bank statements)
  • Medical exam report
  • Police clearance certificate

🔹 5. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

A

Check Eligibility for the Appropriate Visa: You must first decide your pathway.

👉 Example: Skilled worker → Express Entry | Job offer → Work Permit | Province-specific → PNP

What you check here: CRS score (for Express Entry), Work experience category, Language score, and Education equivalency.

👉 Wrong pathway selection reduces chances.

B

Take Language Test & Get ECA: This step is mandatory for most applicants.

What you must do: Take IELTS or CELPIP and get an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA).

👉 ECA verifies your Indian degree in Canadian standards.

👉 Without this, PR application is invalid.

C

Create Profile (Express Entry or Apply for Job): There are two main routes.

Route 1: Express Entry (PR): Create profile on Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada. Enter: Age, Education, Work experience, and Language score. You get a CRS score (points system).

Route 2: Job-Based Work Permit: Apply for jobs via LinkedIn or Indeed Canada. Employer may need LMIA (Labour Market Impact Assessment).

D

Receive Invitation (ITA) or Job Offer: For PR, if your CRS score is high, you receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA). For the job route, the employer provides a job offer + LMIA.

👉 This step determines your success.

E

Submit Application: Now you submit the full application. Upload documents, fill forms online, and pay fees.

👉 Accuracy is critical here.

F

Biometrics & Medical Examination: You must complete fingerprints (biometrics) and a medical test with an approved panel doctor.

👉 Mandatory for all applicants.

G

Background Verification: Authorities verify documents, work experience, and criminal record.

👉 Any mismatch → rejection.

H

Wait for Decision: Processing is done by Canadian authorities. Express Entry takes ~6 months; Work Permit takes 4–12 weeks.

I

Receive Visa / COPR: If approved, PR applicants receive the COPR (Confirmation of Permanent Residence), and work permit applicants receive their visa approval.

👉 This is your final approval.

J

Travel to Canada and Settle: After arrival, get your SIN (Social Insurance Number), open a bank account, and start working.

👉 PR holders can live permanently.

🧠 REALITY CHECK (IMPORTANT)

Canada is one of the best options, but: CRS score competition is high. IELTS score plays a huge role. The job-based route requires LMIA (not easy).

Common mistakes:

  • ❌ Low IELTS score
  • ❌ Wrong CRS calculation
  • ❌ Fake job offers
  • ❌ Incomplete documents
