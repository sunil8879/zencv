🇨🇳 CHINA — WORK IMMIGRATION GUIDE FOR INDIANS

🔹 1. OVERVIEW

China is one of the world’s largest economies and offers opportunities for Indian professionals in teaching (especially English), IT, manufacturing, engineering, trade, and business sectors. The country follows a structured but strict work visa system, requiring employer sponsorship and government approval. Long-term settlement options are limited, but short to medium-term employment is common.

🔹 2. TYPES OF WORK VISAS

1. Z Visa (Work Visa) Most common work visa for legal employment.

Required for all standard jobs. Must be converted into a residence permit within 30 days of arrival in China.

2. R Visa (Talent Visa) For highly skilled professionals and top talent.

Features a fast-track process and is limited to individuals meeting specific high-level criteria.

3. M Visa (Business Visa) For business visits and corporate meetings.

Allows short-term entry but does not permit full-time employment or salary payment from a Chinese source.

4. F Visa For academic exchange and non-commercial visits.

🔹 3. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

  • ✔ Valid job offer from a licensed Chinese employer
  • ✔ Relevant qualifications and professional experience
  • ✔ Bachelor’s degree (mandatory for most Z Visa applications)
  • ✔ Minimum 2 years of relevant post-graduate work experience
  • ✔ Mandatory medical fitness check-up
  • ✔ Clean criminal record (Police Clearance Certificate)

🔹 4. REQUIRED DOCUMENTS

  • 📄 Valid passport & Passport-size photographs
  • 📄 Work Permit Notice issued by Chinese authorities
  • 📄 Job offer / Official employment contract
  • 📄 Attested educational certificates
  • 📄 Attested Police Clearance Certificate (PCC)
  • 📄 Official medical examination report

🔹 5. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

✅ A & B: Eligibility & Job Search
THE MOST IMPORTANT STEP.

Identify the correct visa category and secure a job offer via LinkedIn or niche job portals. Ensure the employer is authorized to hire foreign workers.

✅ C & D: Work Permit Notice

Your employer applies for the Work Permit Notice through Chinese labor authorities. Once approved, you receive the document required for your embassy application.

✅ E & F: Z Visa & Travel

Submit your application at the Chinese Embassy. Once the Z Visa is stamped, you can travel to China to begin your contract.

✅ G, H & I: Arrival Formalities

Undergo a second medical check inside China. Convert your Z Visa into a Residence Permit within 30 days and register your address with the local police station.

🧠 REALITY CHECK

❌ Mandarin language barrier is significant outside of work environments.
❌ China has strict labor regulations; never work without a valid Z Visa.
❌ Permanent Residency (Green Card) is exceptionally difficult to obtain.
❌ Document attestation (Degree, PCC) is a lengthy but mandatory process.
