🇯🇵 JAPAN — WORK IMMIGRATION GUIDE FOR INDIANS

🔹 1. OVERVIEW

Japan is a highly developed economy known for technology, manufacturing, and innovation. It is increasingly opening up to foreign workers due to labor shortages, especially in IT, engineering, healthcare, hospitality, and manufacturing sectors. Japan offers employer-sponsored work visas and structured long-term residence pathways, though language and cultural adaptation are important factors.

🔹 2. TYPES OF WORK VISAS

1. Engineer / Specialist in Humanities Most common visa for IT, engineering, and business.

Requires a job offer from a Japanese employer. Covers professionals in international services, translation, and business management.

2. Specified Skilled Worker (SSW) For labor shortage sectors like hospitality and caregiving.

Divided into SSW Type 1 and Type 2, allowing workers in 14 specific industries to reside and work in Japan.

3. Highly Skilled Professional Visa Points-based system for elite talent.

Offers a fast-track to Permanent Residency (within 1-3 years) for highly qualified researchers and business owners.

4. Intra-Company Transfer For employees transferred within a corporate group.

🔹 3. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

  • ✔ Valid job offer from a registered Japanese employer
  • ✔ Relevant qualifications or proven skills for the role
  • ✔ Japanese language proficiency (JLPT N4-N2 preferred for most roles)
  • ✔ Valid passport & Medical fitness certification
  • ✔ Clean criminal record (Police Clearance Certificate)

🔹 4. REQUIRED DOCUMENTS

  • 📄 Valid passport & Passport-size photographs
  • 📄 Job offer / Official employment contract
  • 📄 Certificate of Eligibility (CoE)
  • 📄 Educational certificates (Degree/Diploma)
  • 📄 Work experience certificates

🔹 5. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

✅ A & B: Eligibility & Job Search THE MOST IMPORTANT STEP.

Identify your visa category. Secure a job offer via LinkedIn, Indeed Japan, or portals like Daijob. Without an employer to sponsor your CoE, the process cannot proceed.

✅ C & D: Certificate of Eligibility (CoE)

Your employer applies for the CoE with the Immigration Bureau in Japan. Once approved and sent to you, it serves as the foundation for your visa application.

✅ E, F & G: Visa Stamping & Travel

Submit your application to the Japanese Embassy with the CoE. Once approved (usually 1-4 weeks), travel to Japan and receive your Residence Card at the airport.

✅ H, I & J: Post-Arrival Registration

Register your address at the local ward office within 14 days. Enroll in the national health insurance system and begin your legal employment.

🧠 REALITY CHECK

❌ Japanese language (JLPT) is often a non-negotiable requirement for social and professional integration.
❌ Work culture is demanding; punctuality and hierarchy are highly valued.
❌ Permanent Residency generally takes 10 years unless you qualify for the High-Skilled points system.
❌ Ensure all degree documents are verified and ready for the CoE application.
