IMMIGRATION PROCESS FOR INDIANS IN NEW ZEALAND

🇳🇿 NEW ZEALAND — WORK IMMIGRATION GUIDE

🔹 1. Overview

New Zealand is an attractive destination for Indian professionals due to its high quality of life, safe environment, and growing demand for skilled workers. Key sectors include healthcare, construction, IT, agriculture, engineering, and trades. New Zealand offers employer-sponsored work visas as well as pathways to Permanent Residency (PR), though policies have become more selective in recent years.

🔹 2. Types of Work Visas

1. Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV): Most common work visa. Requires job offer from an accredited employer. Employer must be approved by immigration authorities.

2. Skilled Migrant Category (SMC): Points-based PR system. For skilled professionals. Requires meeting eligibility criteria.

3. Green List Visa: For occupations in high demand. Fast-track to residence. Includes healthcare, engineering roles.

4. Working Holiday Visa: For young applicants (limited category). Short-term work and travel.

5. Post-Study Work Visa: For students who studied in New Zealand. Allows temporary work.

🔹 3. Eligibility Criteria

  • Valid job offer (for AEWV)
  • Relevant qualifications and work experience
  • English language proficiency (IELTS/PTE)
  • Occupation must meet skill level requirements
  • Health and character requirements
  • Employer must be accredited (for job-based visas)

🔹 4. Required Documents

  • Valid passport
  • Job offer letter
  • Educational certificates
  • Work experience letters
  • English test results (IELTS/PTE)
  • Medical examination report
  • Police clearance certificate

🔹 5. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

A

Check Eligibility for the Appropriate Visa: You must first decide your pathway.

👉 Example: Job-based → AEWV | Skilled PR → SMC | High-demand job → Green List

What you check here: Is your job role in demand? Do you meet qualification requirements? Do you meet English language requirement?

👉 Wrong selection reduces chances.

B

Secure a Job Offer from Accredited Employer: This is the MOST IMPORTANT STEP. An Accredited Employer is a company approved by immigration authorities to hire foreign workers.

What you must do: Apply via LinkedIn, Indeed New Zealand, or Company career websites. Target employers that are accredited and offer visa sponsorship.

👉 Without job offer → AEWV not possible.

C

Employer Completes Job Check: Before hiring you, the employer must prove that no local worker is available and that the job meets wage standards. This is called Job Check approval.

D

Apply for Work Visa: Now you apply via Immigration New Zealand. You must fill the application form, upload documents, and pay the visa fee.

E

Submit Biometrics (if required): Some applicants may need to provide fingerprints and a photograph. This depends on the specific case.

F

Medical & Police Verification: Mandatory steps include a medical examination and police clearance to ensure you meet health and character requirements.

G

Wait for Decision: Authorities process your application. Standard AEWV processing takes 1–3 months; PR pathways take longer.

H

Receive Visa Approval: If approved, you receive a visa grant which confirms your legal entry into the country.

I

Travel to New Zealand: You enter the country and prepare for your professional transition.

J

Start Working and Settle: After arrival, open a bank account, get an IRD number (tax number), and start working. Eligible candidates can later apply for PR.

🧠 REALITY CHECK (IMPORTANT)

New Zealand is a good option, but: Job offer is critical. Employer accreditation is mandatory. Opportunities are fewer compared to bigger countries.

Common mistakes:

  • ❌ Applying without checking employer accreditation
  • ❌ Low IELTS score
  • ❌ Incomplete documents
  • ❌ Fake job offers

Work visa: 1–3 months

Entry-level: NZD 45,000 – 60,000/year

High-demand roles: NZD 100,000+

🔹 9. PR Options: Available via Skilled Migrant Category or Green List pathway.

✔ Pros

High quality of life, Safe and peaceful environment, PR pathways available, Work-life balance.

❌ Cons

Limited job market, Distance from India, Strict immigration policies, Slower processing for PR.

🔹 11. Difficulty Level: Medium

The job offer is the key challenge. The process is structured but highly selective.
