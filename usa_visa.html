IMMIGRATION PROCESS FOR INDIANS IN USA

🇺🇸 UNITED STATES — WORK IMMIGRATION GUIDE

🔹 1. Overview

The United States is one of the top destinations for Indian professionals due to its high salaries, global companies, and advanced industries. Major demand exists in IT, engineering, healthcare, finance, and research. However, the US immigration system is complex and competitive, especially due to visa caps and lottery systems. It offers temporary work visas and pathways to permanent residency (Green Card).

🔹 2. Types of Work Visas

1. H-1B Visa: Most common work visa. For skilled professionals (IT, engineering, finance, etc.). Requires employer sponsorship and is subject to an annual lottery system.

2. L-1 Visa: For intra-company transfer. Employee must work in an overseas branch of the same company. No lottery required.

3. O-1 Visa: For individuals with extraordinary ability. Used in tech, science, arts, sports. High-level achievements required.

4. H-2B Visa: Temporary non-agricultural workers. Short-term or seasonal jobs.

5. F-1 OPT (Work Route via Study): Students study in the US and then work via OPT. Common pathway for freshers.

🔹 3. Eligibility Criteria

  • Relevant bachelor’s degree (minimum for H-1B)
  • Job must be a “specialty occupation”
  • Valid job offer from US employer
  • Employer must sponsor visa
  • Strong academic or professional background
  • English proficiency (no mandatory IELTS, but required for communication)

🔹 4. Required Documents

  • Valid passport
  • Job offer letter
  • Educational certificates (degree, transcripts)
  • Work experience letters
  • Resume/CV
  • Employer petition approval (I-797)
  • Financial documents (if required)

🔹 5. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

A

Check Eligibility for the Appropriate Visa: This is where most people go wrong. You must choose the correct visa type based on your profile.

👉 Example: IT professional → H-1B Visa | Working in MNC → L-1 Visa | Highly talented → O-1 Visa

What you check here: Does your degree match the job role? Is your job a specialty occupation? Do you meet skill and experience requirements?

👉 If not eligible, your case will be rejected later.

B

Secure a Job Offer from a US Employer: This is mandatory. You cannot apply for most US work visas without a job.

What you must do: Apply via job portals (LinkedIn, Indeed US, Company career pages). Target companies that sponsor H-1B visas and have a history of hiring foreign workers.

👉 This step is harder than UK due to global competition.

C

Employer Files Petition (Form I-129): Unlike the UK, you do not apply first. Your employer files a petition with the United States Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS).

What employer does: Files Form I-129, submits your qualifications, and proves the job is valid and meets salary norms.

👉 Without this, you cannot proceed.

D

H-1B Lottery System (If Applicable): This is the biggest challenge. There are limited visas (~85,000/year) and applications exceed the limit.

👉 A random lottery system selects candidates. Even if you are qualified, selection is NOT guaranteed.

👉 This is why many applicants fail.

E

Petition Approval (I-797): If selected and approved, you receive the I-797 Approval Notice. This confirms your visa is approved and you can proceed for stamping.

F

Apply for Visa Stamping: Now you apply for the visa at a US embassy through the U.S. Department of State. You must fill the DS-160 form, upload a photo, and pay the visa fee.

G

Book Biometrics & Interview Appointment: You must schedule two appointments at the US Visa Application Center: 1. Biometrics and 2. Visa interview.

H

Attend Biometrics Appointment: At the VAC, your fingerprints are taken and your photograph is captured. This is a quick process (10–15 minutes).

I

Attend Visa Interview: At the US Embassy/Consulate, an officer asks questions about your job, employer, salary, and background. This is a critical step.

J

Receive Visa and Travel to the US: If approved, you get the visa stamped in your passport. After reaching the US, start working for your sponsoring employer. You can now work legally, open a bank account, and build your career.

🧠 REALITY CHECK (IMPORTANT)

This process is not easy: The lottery system makes it unpredictable. Even qualified candidates may not get selected, and interview rejection is common.

Common mistakes:

  • ❌ Fake consultancies
  • ❌ Fake job offers
  • ❌ Poor interview preparation
  • ❌ Wrong visa selection

🔹 7. Cost Estimate

Visa fee: ~$185
Petition cost: Paid by employer
Misc (travel, docs): Variable

🔹 6. Processing Time:
Lottery + petition: 3–6 months
Stamping: 2–6 weeks

🔹 8. Salary Expectation

IT: $70k – $150k/year
Engineering: $60k – $120k/year
Entry-level: $50k – $80k/year

🔹 9. PR Options:
Apply for Green Card (EB-2/EB-3). Long wait for Indians due to backlog.

✔ Pros

Very high salary, Global career opportunities, Top companies (Google, Microsoft), Strong career growth.

❌ Cons

Lottery-based system, Long Green Card wait time, High competition, Strict policies.

🔹 11. Difficulty Level: Hard

Lottery + sponsorship makes it difficult. Uncertain outcome even if qualified.
