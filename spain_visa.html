🇪🇸 SPAIN — WORK IMMIGRATION GUIDE FOR INDIANS

🔹 1. OVERVIEW

Spain is a popular European destination offering opportunities for Indian workers in tourism, hospitality, construction, agriculture, logistics, IT, and services. Spain has a structured immigration system that includes employer-sponsored work visas, a digital nomad visa, and pathways to long-term residence (PR). However, the job market can be competitive, and knowledge of Spanish is often important.

🔹 2. TYPES OF WORK VISAS

1. Work Visa (Employee Visa) Most common visa for long-term employment.

Requires a job offer from a Spanish employer. The employer must obtain work authorization before you apply for the visa.

2. EU Blue Card For highly skilled professionals.

Designed for specialists with higher salary thresholds and advanced qualifications.

3. Digital Nomad Visa For remote workers earning from outside Spain.

Allows professionals to live in Spain while working for companies located in other countries.

4. Seasonal & Self-Employment For agriculture, tourism, or freelancers.

🔹 3. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

  • ✔ Valid job offer from a registered Spanish employer
  • ✔ Employer must secure official work authorization
  • ✔ Relevant qualifications or skills for the specific position
  • ✔ Basic Spanish language proficiency (highly preferred)
  • ✔ Valid passport & Medical fitness certificate
  • ✔ Clean criminal record (Police Clearance Certificate)

🔹 4. REQUIRED DOCUMENTS

  • 📄 Valid passport & Passport-size photographs
  • 📄 Job offer / Official employment contract
  • 📄 Work authorization issued from Spain
  • 📄 Educational & Work experience certificates
  • 📄 Police clearance certificate (PCC)
  • 📄 Proof of accommodation & Medical certificate

🔹 5. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

✅ A & B: Eligibility & Job Search
THE MOST IMPORTANT STEP.

Identify the correct visa category and secure a job offer via LinkedIn or Spanish career portals. Target employers authorized to hire foreign workers.

✅ C & D: Employer Authorization

The employer applies to Spanish labor authorities. Once the hiring is justified and approved, you receive the authorization document required for your visa application.

✅ E, F & G: Visa Application & Approval

Submit your application at the Spanish Embassy. Attend your interview for verification. Processing typically takes between 1 to 3 months.

✅ H, I & J: Arrival & TIE Card

Travel to Spain and apply for your residence permit (TIE card) within 30 days of arrival. This finalized permit allows you to work and live legally for the long term.

🧠 REALITY CHECK

❌ Spanish language skills are often vital for local recruitment.
❌ Salaries in Spain may be lower compared to Germany or the UK.
❌ The job market is highly competitive with a focus on local and EU talent first.
❌ Bureaucracy can be slow; ensure all documents are complete and translated.

Typically 1–3 months for visa and permit processing.
