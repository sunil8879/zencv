IMMIGRATION PROCESS FOR INDIANS IN AUSTRALIA

🇦🇺 AUSTRALIA — WORK IMMIGRATION GUIDE

🔹 1. Overview

Australia is a highly preferred destination for Indian professionals due to its strong economy, high salaries, and transparent immigration system. There is strong demand in IT, healthcare, engineering, construction, mining, and education sectors. Australia offers both temporary work visas and direct Permanent Residency (PR) pathways through a points-based system.

🔹 2. Types of Work Visas

1. Temporary Skill Shortage Visa (Subclass 482): Most common employer-sponsored visa. Requires job offer from Australian employer. Valid up to 4 years.

2. Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189): Points-based PR visa. No job offer required. Direct Permanent Residency.

3. Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190): State-sponsored PR visa. Requires nomination by an Australian state.

4. Skilled Work Regional Visa (Subclass 491): Regional area visa. Points-based. Leads to PR after few years.

5. Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485): For students who studied in Australia. Allows temporary work.

🔹 3. Eligibility Criteria

  • Relevant educational qualifications
  • Occupation must be on Skilled Occupation List
  • Minimum IELTS/PTE score
  • Work experience (preferred)
  • Points-based eligibility (age, education, experience)
  • Health and character requirements

🔹 4. Required Documents

  • Valid passport
  • Educational certificates
  • Skill assessment report
  • IELTS/PTE score
  • Work experience letters
  • Expression of Interest (EOI) details
  • Medical examination report
  • Police clearance certificate

🔹 5. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

A

Check Eligibility for the Appropriate Visa: You must first decide your pathway.

👉 Example: No job → Subclass 189 (PR) | State support → Subclass 190 | Job offer → Subclass 482

What you check here: Is your occupation on Skilled Occupation List? Do you meet minimum points (65+)? Do you meet English requirement?

👉 Wrong choice reduces chances significantly.

B

Get Skill Assessment: This is a mandatory step. Your qualifications are evaluated by an Australian authority to confirm your job role matches Australian standards.

👉 Without skill assessment → cannot apply.

C

Take English Language Test: You must take IELTS or PTE. The minimum score varies by visa; however, a higher score equals more points.

👉 Score directly impacts your selection chances.

D

Submit Expression of Interest (EOI): Now you create a profile via the Department of Home Affairs Australia. You enter Age, Education, Work experience, and English score. The system calculates your points score.

E

Receive Invitation to Apply (ITA): If your score is competitive, you receive an invitation. Higher points = higher chances.

F

Submit Visa Application: Now you submit the full application. Upload documents, pay the visa fee, and submit. Accuracy is critical.

G

Medical & Police Verification: You must complete a medical examination and police clearance. This is mandatory for all applicants.

H

Wait for Decision: The application is processed by authorities. PR visas take 6–12 months; Work visas take 1–3 months.

I

Receive Visa Grant: If approved, you receive a visa grant letter which confirms your legal entry.

J

Travel to Australia and Start Work: After arrival, apply for a Tax File Number (TFN), open a bank account, and start working. PR holders can live permanently.
