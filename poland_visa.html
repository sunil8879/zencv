🇵🇱 POLAND — WORK IMMIGRATION GUIDE FOR INDIANS

🔹 1. OVERVIEW

Poland is emerging as a popular destination for Indian workers due to its growing economy, increasing demand for foreign labor, and relatively easier visa process compared to Western Europe. Opportunities are strong in manufacturing, logistics, IT, construction, warehousing, and hospitality. Poland offers employer-sponsored work visas and pathways to long-term residence within the European Union.

🔹 2. TYPES OF WORK VISAS

1. National Work Visa (Type D) Most common work visa for long-term employment.

Requires an official job offer from a Polish employer and a valid work permit.

2. EU Blue Card For highly skilled professionals.

Requires higher salary thresholds and leads directly to long-term residence options.

3. Seasonal Work Visa Short-term jobs (agriculture, tourism).

Valid for a limited duration, specifically for seasonal sectors.

4. Temporary Residence Permit Linked to employment after entry.

Allows for a longer stay and legal residency while working in Poland.

🔹 3. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

  • ✔ Valid job offer from Polish employer
  • ✔ Relevant qualifications or skills for the role
  • ✔ Employer must obtain a work permit from local authorities
  • ✔ Basic English or Polish knowledge (preferred)
  • ✔ Valid passport & Clean criminal record

🔹 4. REQUIRED DOCUMENTS

  • 📄 Valid passport & Passport-size photographs
  • 📄 Job offer letter / Employment contract
  • 📄 Work permit issued by the Polish employer
  • 📄 Educational and Work experience certificates
  • 📄 Medical insurance & Proof of accommodation

🔹 5. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

Step A & B
Job Search & Eligibility

Secure a job offer via LinkedIn or Indeed Poland. This is the MOST IMPORTANT STEP. Ensure your employer can provide work permit support.

Step C & D
Work Permit Approval

The employer applies for the permit in Poland. Once approved, you receive the official document required for your visa application.

Step E, F & G
Visa Application & Interview

Apply for the National Visa (Type D) at the Polish Embassy. Attend your appointment for document verification. Processing takes 2–8 weeks.

Step H, I & J
Arrival & Residence

Enter Poland, start your job, and apply for a Temporary Residence Permit to secure your long-term legal stay.

🧠 REALITY CHECK

❌ Fake job offers are common; always verify employers.
❌ Salaries may be lower than in Western Europe.
❌ Language barriers can exist outside major cities.

PR Options
