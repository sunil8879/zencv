🇰🇼 KUWAIT — WORK IMMIGRATION GUIDE FOR INDIANS

🔹 1. OVERVIEW

Kuwait is a popular destination for Indian workers due to tax-free income, demand for labor, and employer-driven visa processes. Opportunities are available in construction, oil & gas, healthcare, hospitality, retail, and domestic work. Kuwait offers employer-sponsored work visas under a sponsorship system, with limited long-term settlement options.

🔹 2. TYPES OF WORK VISAS

1. Work Visa (Article 18) Most common work visa for private sector employees.

Requires employer sponsorship and is the standard pathway for most Indian professionals.

2. Government Work Visa (Article 17) For employees working in the government sector.

Requires direct sponsorship from a Kuwaiti government entity.

3. Domestic Worker Visa (Article 20) For housemaids, drivers, and household staff.

Sponsored by individual Kuwaiti employers.

4. Temporary Work Permit For short-term projects and limited duration roles.

🔹 3. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

  • ✔ Valid job offer from a Kuwaiti employer
  • ✔ Age between 21–60 years (varies by job role)
  • ✔ Relevant skills or professional qualifications
  • ✔ Mandatory medical fitness certification
  • ✔ Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • ✔ Clean criminal record (Police Clearance Certificate)

🔹 4. REQUIRED DOCUMENTS

  • 📄 Valid passport & Passport-size photographs
  • 📄 Job offer / Official employment contract
  • 📄 Work permit approval from Kuwait authorities
  • 📄 Attested educational certificates
  • 📄 Medical fitness certificate & Police clearance certificate

🔹 5. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

✅ A & B: Eligibility & Job Search THE MOST IMPORTANT STEP.

Secure a job offer from a legally registered Kuwaiti employer. Without a job offer and sponsorship, the process cannot begin.

✅ C & D: Employer Actions

The employer applies for work permit approval and submits the contract. Once government verification is complete, you receive the official work permit document.

✅ E & F: Visa & Medicals

Apply for the Entry Visa through the Kuwait Embassy. Complete your mandatory medical examination and obtain your Police Clearance Certificate (PCC).

✅ G, H & I: Arrival & Civil ID

Enter Kuwait on your entry visa. Your employer then applies for your Iqama (Residence Permit) and your Civil ID card, which is required for banking and legal identity.

🧠 REALITY CHECK

❌ Beware of fake recruitment agents; always verify the employer.
❌ Never work on a visit visa; it carries strict legal penalties.
❌ The process is fully employer-controlled with no current PR pathway.
